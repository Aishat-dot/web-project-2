Treaties

What Is A Treaty?

A treaty is an legal agreement between two or more groups that explains what each group has to do and what they have the right to do. Treaties can be made between governments, communities, countries, or any other groups.

Many treaties where created all across Canada, but only five are inside Manitoba. Treaties 1 to 5 are all apart of Manitoba in some way. Although there are many treaties to study, we will only be covering Treaty 1. First though, we will be briefly explaing each Treaty that is within Manitoba.

Treaty 1

Treaty 1 was made between the First Nations of Long Plain, Sagkeeng, Brokenhead Ojibway, Sandy Bay Ojibway, Swan Lake, Peguis, Roseau River Anishinaabe and the Canadian government on August 3, 1871 at Lower Fort Garry, and is also sometimes known as the Stone Fort Treaty.

Treaty 2

Treaty 2 was made between the First Nations of Keeseekowenin, O-Chi-Chak-Ko-Sipi, Ebb and Flow, Lake Manitoba, Lake St. Martin, Little Saskatchewan, Skownan, Dauphin River, Pinaymootang and the Canadian government on August 21, 1871 at Manitoba House, now located near Kinosota, Manitoba.

Treaty 3

Treaty 3 was made in October 1873, at Fort Frances, Northwest Angle. The area of this Treaty is mostly within the borders of Ontario, however, Manitoba’s Buffalo Point First Nation is a party to Treaty No. 3.

Treaty 4

Treaty 4 was made in September 15 & 21, 1874 at Fort Qu’Appelle, Fort Ellice, Saskatchewan. The larger part of the Treaty area is in Saskatchewan, however, a small part of western Manitoba is Treaty 4 land.

Treaty 5

Treaty 5 was made by the largest number of First Nation communities in Manitoba at different places and times. The first concluding of Treaty 5 occurred at Berens River in 1875. Manitoba communities that are under the rules and benefits of Treaty 5 include: The Pas, Wabowden, and Gypsumville.
